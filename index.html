History and upbringing

Al Ahly Sporting Club it is knownin short as Al-Ahly Club is a professional Egyptian sports club that plays in the Egyptian Premier League headquartered in Cairo and is the only club in Egypt besides Zamalek that has not been relegated to the second division. Al-Ahly Club was founded on April 24 1907 by many dignitaries in Egypt such as Omar Lotfi Bey Amin Sami Pasha, Mustafa Kamel and Talaat Harb. Headed by the Englishman Michael Ennis, and in 1909 the first football stadium was established in the club’s history and was named at the time (Al Hosh), and this stadium developed until it became named after him. Al-Ahly fans nicknamed the club several nicknames namely the Red Devils, the Red Genie the Red Castle and the National Club. Al-Ahly was chosen as the best African club in the twentieth century according to the decisionofthe Confederation of African Football issued in 2000, and was also chosen as the best club in the world according to the International Federation of History And football statistics for the month of June 2006 and July 2007 and was also chosen as the best African club in the years 2005, 2006, 2008, 2012 and 2013, and also received the Globe Soccer award for the most decorated club in the Middle East in 2020 and also obtained the certificate of standards (ISO). 9001:2015) in 2021, and Al-Ahly holds (ISO). 9001:2015) in 2021 and Al-Ahly
